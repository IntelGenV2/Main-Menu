## High-Level Concept

**System Revival** is a retro-styled, first-person, motherboard-crawling action game where you repair a 90s PC from the inside.

- You play as a tiny **diagnostic bot** walking along traces, between chips, and through a labyrinth of components.
- The **motherboard is the main map** of your own "Local Machine."
- Repairing components literally upgrades:
  - Graphics (GPU, video ports)
  - Audio (sound card)
  - Stability & speed (CPU, RAM, HDD/SSD, PSU, cooling)
  - Communication (network hardware, USB, Parallel, Serial)
- There are **two layers**:
  1. **Local Machine** – single-player, persistent, fixed layout. (see Local Machine.md)
  2. **DataNET** – multiplayer mode: unstable remote systems, procedural runs. (see DataNET.md)
- Endgame: trace the infection to its source inside the USB core and defeat **Entrocore**, a "USB killer–class" virus, in a hidden final dungeon. Fixing USB is the last repair.
